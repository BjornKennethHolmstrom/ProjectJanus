# Organizational Design

*Creating Life-Affirming Organizations: Applying Janus Principles to Workplace Design*

---

## Purpose

This document applies the Project Janus integrated human model to organizational design. Rather than treating organizations as mechanical systems for productivity, we approach them as **living ecosystems** that can either support or hinder human development, meaning-making, and flourishing across all domains.

## Core Philosophical Shift

### From Mechanical to Living Organizations
**Traditional**: Organizations as machines for efficient production
**Janus**: Organizations as living systems that support human development

### From Extraction to Cultivation  
**Traditional**: Extract maximum productivity from human resources
**Janus**: Cultivate human potential while achieving collective purposes

### From Control to Empowerment
**Traditional**: Control behavior through rules and incentives
**Janus**: Empower authentic contribution through meaning and values

```python
class OrganizationalFlourishingMetrics:
    def comprehensive_evaluation(self):

        return {
            'biological_flourishing': {
                'physical_health_index': 'Aggregate physical wellbeing measures',
                'energy_vitality_score': 'Sustained energy and recovery capacity',
                'burnout_prevention_metrics': 'Absence of exhaustion and cynicism'
            },
            'cognitive_flourishing': {
                'learning_growth_rate': 'Pace of skill and knowledge development',
                'innovation_capacity': 'Ability to generate and implement new ideas',
                'wisdom_application': 'Quality of decision-making and judgment'
            },
            'emotional_flourishing': {
                'emotional_vitality': 'Range and authenticity of emotional expression',
                'relational_depth': 'Quality and authenticity of relationships',
                'psychological_safety': 'Freedom to take interpersonal risks'
            },
            'social_flourishing': {
                'community_cohesion': 'Sense of belonging and mutual care',
                'collaborative_efficacy': 'Effectiveness of working together',
                'justice_experience': 'Perception of fairness and equity'
            },
            'existential_flourishing': {
                'purpose_alignment': 'Connection between work and meaningful purpose',
                'contribution_clarity': 'Understanding of meaningful impact',
                'transcendence_opportunities': 'Chances to serve beyond self-interest'
            }
        }
    
    def calculate_overall_flourishing_index(self, metric_data):
        """Compute composite organizational flourishing score"""
        domain_scores = {}
        for domain, metrics in metric_data.items():
            domain_score = np.mean([score for score in metrics.values()])
            domain_scores[domain] = domain_score
        
        # Weight existential and social domains slightly higher for holistic health
        weights = {
            'biological_flourishing': 0.15,
            'cognitive_flourishing': 0.15,
            'emotional_flourishing': 0.15,
            'social_flourishing': 0.20,
            'existential_flourishing': 0.20
        }
        
        overall_score = sum(domain_scores[domain] * weight 
                          for domain, weight in weights.items())
        return overall_score
```

## The Bigger Vision

Janus-informed organizational design represents a fundamental shift from seeing organizations as machines for production to understanding them as **living ecosystems for human development**. The ultimate measure of organizational success becomes not just profitability or efficiency, but the degree to which the organization supports human flourishing across all domains of experience.

When organizations become contexts that support meaning-making, developmental growth, authentic relationship, and purposeful contribution, they become not just successful businesses but **contributors to human evolution** - creating the conditions for the emergence of more wise, compassionate, and integrated human beings.

This approach suggests that the organizations of the future will be judged not by what they extract from humans, but by what they help humans become.
