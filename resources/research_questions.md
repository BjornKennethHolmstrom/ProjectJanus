# Research Questions

*Guiding Inquiry for the Next Generation of Human Understanding*

---

## Purpose

This document outlines the fundamental research questions that Project Janus seeks to address. These questions span multiple disciplines and represent the frontiers of our understanding about what it means to be human in complex, often pathological, social systems.

## Foundational Philosophical Questions

### 1. The Nature of Human Nature
- What does it mean to model humans as integrated wholes rather than collections of parts?
- How do we distinguish between healthy human suffering and pathological distress caused by toxic systems?
- What are the universal aspects of human experience vs. culturally constructed ones?
- How do we model human potential while acknowledging systemic constraints?

### 2. Ethics of Human Modeling
- What ethical frameworks should guide the creation of comprehensive human models?
- How do we avoid reductionism while still creating computationally tractable models?
- What responsibilities come with understanding human nature at this depth?
- How do we ensure these models serve human flourishing rather than control?

### 3. Systemic vs. Individual Perspectives
- **Crucial Question**: How do we distinguish between individual pathology and sane responses to pathological systems?
- When is "treatment" of individuals actually complicity in maintaining harmful systems?
- How can our models account for the ways social structures create and maintain psychological suffering?
- What would mental health look like in truly life-affirming social systems?

## Core Modeling Research Questions

### Multi-Domain Integration
- What are the fundamental mechanisms of cross-domain influence and integration?
- How do disruptions in one domain propagate through the entire system?
- What constitutes healthy integration vs. pathological fragmentation?
- How do we model the emergent properties that arise from domain interactions?

### Developmental Trajectories  
- What are the universal patterns of human development across all domains?
- How do developmental pathways differ across cultures and contexts?
- What conditions support healthy development vs. those that create distortion?
- How can we model stage transitions and transformative experiences?

### Meaning-Making Systems
- How do humans construct and maintain meaning in different contexts?
- What makes meaning systems resilient or fragile?
- How do meaning crises trigger transformation vs. pathology?
- What are the relationships between personal meaning and cultural narratives?

## Critical Systemic Questions

### Society as Cause of Suffering
- **Central Question**: Which aspects of modern social systems inherently generate psychological distress?
- How do economic systems that prioritize growth over wellbeing affect human psychology?
- What psychological costs are imposed by social structures that undermine community and belonging?
- How do educational systems that standardize and rank impact developing minds?

### The Pathology-Normalcy Boundary
- When does "successful adaptation" to a pathological system become its own form of illness?
- How do we identify when entire cultures have normalized pathological patterns?
- What would psychological assessment look like if it evaluated system health rather than individual conformity?
- How can we distinguish between personal healing needs and necessary resistance to harmful systems?

### Transformation at Scale
- What would mental health systems look like if they addressed systemic causes rather than individual symptoms?
- How can we model the relationship between personal transformation and social change?
- What are the leverage points for creating more psychologically wise social systems?
- How do we avoid the individualization of suffering that characterizes much contemporary mental health?

## Methodological Challenges

### Multi-Disciplinary Integration
- How do we integrate qualitative depth with quantitative rigor in human modeling?
- What research methods can capture the complexity of cross-domain interactions?
- How do we study meaning and values without reducing them to measurable variables?
- What role does researcher reflexivity play in studying human experience?

### Modeling Complex Systems
- How do we model humans as complex adaptive systems while maintaining practical utility?
- What computational approaches can handle the multi-scale nature of human experience?
- How do we represent the qualitative dimensions of experience in formal models?
- What validation methods are appropriate for integrated human models?

### Ethical Research Practices
- How do we conduct research that honors human dignity and complexity?
- What safeguards prevent the misuse of comprehensive human models?
- How do we ensure research benefits the communities being studied?
- What forms of knowledge production serve human liberation rather than control?

## Priority Research Projects
